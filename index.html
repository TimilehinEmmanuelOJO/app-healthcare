Preserving the integrity of
healthcare commodities.

According to the World Health Organization, 19.4 million infants
globally do not have access to life saving vaccines largely due to a
lack of inadequate cold chain equipment.

Cold Chain Monitoring for Healthcare
divider
Most pharmaceutical products like vaccines, diluents etc. are temperature
sensitive. They require special handling from the moment they are manufactured to when they are stored, transported and distributed to the final consumer. For them to remain potent, temperature sensitive pharmaceuticals must be kept within the cold chain at the manufacturers recommended temperature.

Therefore it is compulsory to both maintain the cold chain using a network of cold stores, freezers and other refrigerated systems, and to monitor the condition of pharmaceuticals during their storage, transport and distribution, in real time.

With real-time cold chain monitoring, reports and actionable data is generated immediately a temperature excursion is detected so designated persons at each level of the cold chain can take proactive decisions that reduce damages or loss of integrity.
